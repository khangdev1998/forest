VIETNAM FOREST RESOURCES MUSEUM

The museum has been keeping a set of specimens on forest resources including: Animals (with a variety of classes: birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians, ...), plants and insects. Collected from all regions of the country, with many rare and endangered species listed in the Vietnam Red Book. They are carefully preserved and kept in a building with an area of ​​more than one thousand square meters. The Vietnam Forest Resources Museum not only has that precious dry specimen set but also a living resource specimen set. It is a botanical garden of over 3 hectares attached to the Museum's Gallery. The garden is full of plant species typical for the typical forest types of our country. With the diversity and abundance of natural resources bestowed, the Vietnam Forest Resources Museum is like a miniature forest located in the heart of the capital.
